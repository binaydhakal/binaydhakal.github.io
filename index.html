§ 01 — About

A brief introduction

I build AI systems that have to actually work in production — from multilingual sentiment pipelines to transformer-based medical imaging models — and I treat the boundary between research and shipping engineering as a place to make interesting things, not a wall.

My day-to-day sits between a research lab and an IDE. At the University of New Haven, I work on computational biology and business modeling using dimensionality reduction, autoencoders, and variational methods. Before that, I spent three years as a Senior Software Engineer at Danphe Software Labs, shipping full-stack products and embedding ML into them — cyber-attack detection with SVM/CNN/RNN ensembles, workplace-analytics engines built on questionnaires, MERN stacks fronted by real users.

I read papers. I ship code. I care about the craft of both. If the gap between them feels wide, that's usually where the interesting problems live.
