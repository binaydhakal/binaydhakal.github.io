§ 02 — Yanib

The company I'm building.

Yanib is an AI-powered release-notes platform for dev teams and individual engineers — the missing piece between "I shipped something" and "the world knows about it."

Connect a GitHub or GitLab repo and Yanib takes it from there. It watches your PRs and commits in the background, and on every new tag — or fully on autopilot if you prefer, with zero clicks — drafts a clean, structured changelog in seconds. Review and tweak if you want, then Yanib publishes everywhere at once: GitHub Releases, a public changelog page, Slack, Discord, email subscribers, and an embeddable widget.

For individuals, Yanib goes further. Every user gets a public /u/username profile — with an AI-written tagline, a 365-day shipping heatmap, pinned featured releases, and a grouped timeline of everything you've ever shipped. Plus developer profile cards for READMEs, shipping-stats badges, year-in-review pages, and embeddable feeds for your portfolio.
